Excited to share that I recently participated in the WiDS Worldwide Global Datathon 2026 on Kaggle as an AI Fellow with Break Through Tech!

This year’s challenge focused on predicting wildfire risk to nearby communities. Using data from only the first five hours after ignition, participants built models to estimate the probability that a fire could threaten an evacuation zone within 12, 24, 48, and 72 hours.

For this project, I worked with Python and machine learning, experimenting with tree-based models, cross-validation, and techniques inspired by survival analysis to improve model performance and probability calibration. Through multiple submissions, I iterated and tuned the model to improve its results.
This experience reinforced the importance of iteration and persistence in data science and showed how machine learning can support real-world decision making.

Grateful to Women in Data Science (WiDS), Break Through Tech, and Watch Duty for organizing such an impactful challenge!
